Method

We use cohomCalg to compute the dimension of line bundle cohomologies. This is achieved with the following methods.

all_cohomologies â Method
all_cohomologies(l::ToricLineBundle)

Computes the dimension of all sheaf cohomologies of the toric line bundle l by use of the cohomCalg algorithm Ralph Blumenhagen, Benjamin Jurke, Thorsten Rahn, Helmut Roschy (2010), [cohomCalg:Implementation(@cite), Helmut Roschy, Thorsten Rahn (2010), Shin-Yao Jow (2011), Ralph Blumenhagen, Benjamin Jurke, Thorsten Rahn, Helmut Roschy (2012).

Examples

julia> dP3 = del_pezzo(3)
A normal, non-affine, smooth, projective, gorenstein, fano, 2-dimensional toric variety without torusfactor

julia> all_cohomologies(ToricLineBundle(dP3, [1,2,3,4]))
3-element Vector{fmpz}:
 11
 0
 0
source
cohomology â Method
cohomology(l::ToricLineBundle, i::Int)

Computes the dimension of the i-th sheaf cohomology of the toric line bundle l by use of the cohomCalg algorithm Ralph Blumenhagen, Benjamin Jurke, Thorsten Rahn, Helmut Roschy (2010), [cohomCalg:Implementation(@cite), Helmut Roschy, Thorsten Rahn (2010), Shin-Yao Jow (2011), Ralph Blumenhagen, Benjamin Jurke, Thorsten Rahn, Helmut Roschy (2012).

Examples

julia> dP3 = del_pezzo(3)
A normal, non-affine, smooth, projective, gorenstein, fano, 2-dimensional toric variety without torusfactor

julia> cohomology(ToricLineBundle(dP3, [1,2,3,4]), 0)
11
